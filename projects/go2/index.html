Overview

This project trains a Unitree Go2 quadruped to walk on command using deep reinforcement learning inside NVIDIA Isaac Lab. Starting from a minimal DirectRLEnv baseline that barely tracks a velocity command, the work turns it into a stable, natural-gaited walker through a custom torque-level controller, a ten-term shaped reward, and a gait-clock formulation — then validates the policy in simulation. Training runs across 4096 GPU-parallel environments on NYU's Greene HPC cluster via SLURM burst compute.

What was actually built: a from-scratch PD torque controller, action-rate (1st + 2nd derivative) smoothing, a Raibert-heuristic foot-placement reward driven by a von-Mises gait clock, foot-clearance and contact-force shaping from the contact sensor, posture/orientation penalties, and early-termination criteria — all layered onto velocity tracking.

The control loop

Rather than letting the physics engine's implicit PD controller drive the joints, the environment disables it (stiffness=0, damping=0) and implements its own torque-level PD controller. The policy outputs joint-position offsets; these become desired positions, and torques are computed and clipped each physics step:

desired_joint_pos = action_scale * action + default_joint_pos
torques = clip(Kp * (desired_joint_pos - joint_pos) - Kd * joint_vel,
               -torque_limit, +torque_limit)
robot.set_joint_effort_target(torques)

Gains: Kp = 20.0, Kd = 0.5, torque clip ±100 N·m, action scale 0.25. The sim steps at 200 Hz with a control decimation of 4 (policy at 50 Hz).

Reward shaping (10 terms)

A naive velocity-tracking reward produces a robot that lurches and falls. The reward function combines ten terms — two positive tracking rewards and eight shaping penalties — each implemented against robot.data and the contact sensor:

Reward termScaleWhat it does
track_lin_vel_xy_exp+1.0exp(−err/0.25) on commanded XY linear velocity
track_ang_vel_z_exp+0.5exp(−err/0.25) on commanded yaw rate
raibert_heuristic−10.0foot placement vs. Raibert target in body frame
feet_clearance−30.0swing-phase foot height vs. 8 cm clearance target
tracking_contacts_shaped_force+4.0penalize contact force when a foot should be in swing
orient−5.0keep base flat (projected gravity XY → 0)
rew_action_rate−0.11st + 2nd derivative of actions (anti-jerk)
lin_vel_z−0.02penalize vertical bounce
ang_vel_xy−0.001penalize roll/pitch rate
dof_vel−0.0001penalize high joint velocities

Gait clock & the Raibert heuristic

A gait clock advances at 3 Hz with a trot phase offset of 0.5 between diagonal foot pairs. Four clock_inputs (sines of the per-foot phases) are fed into the policy observation so it can anticipate the gait. A von-Mises smoothing (κ = 0.07) turns the discrete stance/swing schedule into smooth desired_contact_states, which drive both the foot-clearance and contact-force rewards. The Raibert heuristic computes where each foot should land — from a nominal stance (width 0.25 m, length 0.45 m) plus velocity-dependent offsets — and penalizes the squared error against the actual footstep positions transformed into the body frame.

Observations, actions & terminations

  • Observation (52-D): base linear & angular velocity, projected gravity, the 3-D velocity command, joint positions (relative to default), joint velocities, last actions, and the 4 gait-clock inputs.
  • Action (12-D): joint-position offsets for the 12 leg joints (hip / thigh / calf × 4).
  • Early termination: base contact force > 1 N, robot flipped upside-down (projected gravity z > 0), or base height < 0.20 m — episodes end early so the policy quickly learns to stay up. Episodes otherwise run 20 s.
  • Commands: resampled uniformly in [−1, 1] for vx, vy, and yaw rate on each reset.

Training (PPO / rsl_rl)

The policy is trained with PPO via the rsl_rl framework: an actor-critic MLP (hidden dims [256, 128, 64], ELU, with observation normalization), 32 steps per env across 4096 envs, 5 learning epochs / 4 minibatches per update, learning rate 3e-4 with an adaptive KL schedule (target KL 0.008), γ = 0.99, λ = 0.95, clip 0.2, for up to 500 iterations. Each run emits TensorBoard scalars, model_[epoch].pt checkpoints, the exact config as YAML, and a rollout video.

Robustness experiments

Across runs, torque and friction randomization was introduced to push the policy toward a controller that doesn't overfit to the simulator's perfect physics — a step toward sim-to-real transfer. The before/after pair above (133345 → 133736) shows the effect directly, with the later refined run (134475) producing the cleanest, most consistent stepping.

Tech stack

  • NVIDIA Isaac Lab / Isaac Sim — GPU-accelerated robotics simulation (DirectRLEnv, 4096 parallel envs).
  • PPO (rsl_rl) — on-policy deep RL with an actor-critic MLP.
  • PyTorch — all reward, gait-clock, and controller math, fully vectorized across environments.
  • Contact sensor — per-foot net contact forces for the force-tracking and termination logic.
  • SLURM on NYU Greene HPC — burst compute, job scheduling, and checkpointing.
  • TensorBoard — reward-curve, loss, and episode-length tracking per run.

Outcome

The deliverable is a trained Go2 walking policy that goes well beyond raw velocity tracking — a torque-controlled, gait-clocked walker with shaped foot placement, posture stabilization, and smooth actions — together with a set of benchmarked training runs and the rollout videos above demonstrating the learned gait.
