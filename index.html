Home Safety System – Fall Detection & Smart Stove Control

A wearable safety system that protects elderly individuals living alone, built for the Mechatronics course at NYU Tandon. A pocket-worn unit reads a Memsic 2125 dual-axis accelerometer through pulse-width sampling on a BASIC Stamp 2 microcontroller, detecting falls as calibrated deviations from a per-session baseline. On detection it opens a 30-second cancel window (20 s silent + 10 s buzzer warning); if not cancelled, it commands the user's phone over an RN-42 Bluetooth link to text GPS coordinates and a street address to an emergency contact, while a second BS2 — wired into a rewired 15 A electric stovetop through a Solid State Relay — cuts power to the stove. Includes a soft-reset button, a hardware kill switch, and status LEDs; a full bill-of-materials analysis showed the ~$404 prototype dropping below $100 at production scale.

Sprout – Autonomous Greenhouse Plant Care System

Sprout is an autonomous mechatronic system developed for ROB-GY 6103: Advanced Mechatronics at NYU Tandon School of Engineering. It automates the care of greenhouse plants through a multi-bot architecture driven by real-time soil sensor data. A water bot navigates a 3×2 plant grid using IR line-following, reads soil moisture via capacitive sensors connected to an Arduino, and dispenses precise amounts of water through a solenoid valve based on soil profiles built from gravimetric analysis. A second NPK bot autonomously measures nitrogen, phosphorus, and potassium levels using an RS-485 Modbus sensor, then dispenses compost via a servo-actuated mechanism. A Raspberry Pi camera bot captures plant images served through a Flask web server with AI-based plant identification. Inter-device communication is handled via ESP-NOW protocol between ESP32 modules, enabling fully wireless, autonomous coordination across all three bots with no manual intervention.

Breez – BiPAP Non-Invasive Ventilator with Sensor Fusion & BLE Telemetry

A proof-of-concept non-invasive ventilator built on BiPAP (Bilevel Positive Airway Pressure) principles for post-COVID respiratory rehabilitation and sleep-apnea support, developed for the Advanced Mechatronics course at NYU Tandon. Air is delivered from an Ambu bag by a stepper-driven pump whose speed is set in a real-time closed loop from fused biomedical sensing: a MAX30102 pulse oximeter (SpO₂ + heart rate via red/IR photoplethysmography), a ScioSense ENS160 metal-oxide CO₂/air-quality sensor, and dual piezoresistive pressure sensors in the mask and Ambu bag — all sharing one I²C bus with per-device addressing. Two Arduino UNOs split the work master/slave over I²C: the master evaluates physiological thresholds (heart rate, CO₂, pressure) and flags the slave, which drives the pump through an A4988 stepper driver. Every reading streams over an RN-42/HM-10 Bluetooth Low Energy link to a companion Android app showing live SpO₂, CO₂, and BPM — turning the device into a telemedical, remotely-monitored ventilator.

Go2 RL Locomotion – Training a Unitree Quadruped to Walk in Isaac Lab

A deep reinforcement learning project (ROB-GY 6323, NYU) that trains a Unitree Go2 quadruped to walk inside NVIDIA Isaac Lab. Using PPO across thousands of GPU-parallel environments on NYU's Greene HPC cluster, the policy is shaped through principled reward terms — velocity tracking, posture stabilization, foot clearance, slip minimization, and smooth actions — and made robust through domain randomization with randomized masses, friction, and external push disturbances for sim-to-real transfer. The full pipeline runs on SLURM, producing TensorBoard metrics, model checkpoints, and rollout videos of the trained gait.

ros2_shm_fanout – A Shared-Memory Transport for High-Fan-Out Perception

While running a multi-camera perception stack in NVIDIA Isaac Sim, ROS 2's default transport became the bottleneck: with N camera streams and K subscribers, every frame was serialized and copied per consumer, so load scaled as N×K — pinning the CPU and stalling the simulator itself. I built a same-host shared-memory transport that writes one copy of each frame into /dev/shm and signals readers with a single futex wake; every consumer maps the same physical pages and reads in place. Benchmarked against FastDDS, CycloneDDS+iceoryx, and ros2_shm_msgs using Apex.AI's performance_test methodology, it delivered ~8× less CPU per consumer, flat RAM regardless of subscriber count, and was the only transport to hold ~30 fps with zero frame loss out to 64 consumers. It's type-agnostic (all ~330 ROS message types) and ROS-agnostic — any process that can mmap a file can read the stream, no ROS required.

Satellite Constellation Design for Space-Based Wireless Power Transfer

A complete constellation-design pipeline that answers a concrete question: where do you place ground stations, which orbits do you fly, and what is the fewest satellites that keeps every station powered? Built for ROB-GY 7863 (Space Robotics, NYU), it places 50 ground rectennas by running an Earth land/ocean map through K-Means + Voronoi segmentation, propagates a grid of candidate orbits and computes satellite-to-rectenna visibility fully vectorized on the GPU (PyTorch/CUDA) across a (1183 × 50 × time) tensor, then selects the minimum constellation with a mixed-integer linear program (PuLP/CBC) under per-station coverage constraints — with an independent verifier re-checking every constraint. Underneath sits a from-scratch 12-DOF rigid-body spacecraft model in dual Newton–Euler and Lagrangian formulations, cross-validated against MuJoCo. The whole system is visualized in NVIDIA Isaac Sim with animated multi-hop power beams.
